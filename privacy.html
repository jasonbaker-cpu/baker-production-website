Privacy Policy

Last updated: 27 April 2026

Note: This is a basic placeholder. Before launching, have this reviewed by a lawyer or use a service like Termly or iubenda to generate one compliant with the Australian Privacy Act 1988.

1. Who we are

This site is operated by Baker Production ("we", "us", "our"), a Perth-based property photography business. If you have any questions about this policy, contact us at admin@bakerproduction.com.

2. What information we collect

When you book a shoot or contact us, we may collect:

  • Your name, email address, and phone number
  • The property address for the shoot
  • Payment information (processed by our payment provider — we do not store card details)
  • Any messages or correspondence you send us

3. How we use your information

We use your information to:

  • Schedule and deliver the services you've booked
  • Communicate with you about your booking
  • Process payments
  • Send occasional updates about our services (you can opt out at any time)

4. Who we share it with

We don't sell your information. We share it only with service providers who help us run the business (e.g. payment processors, scheduling tools, email providers). All are bound by their own privacy obligations.

5. Cookies and analytics

We may use cookies and analytics tools (e.g. Google Analytics) to understand how visitors use our site. You can disable cookies in your browser settings.

6. Your rights

Under the Australian Privacy Act, you have the right to access the personal information we hold about you, request corrections, or ask us to delete it. Email admin@bakerproduction.com to make a request.

7. Data security

We take reasonable steps to protect your information. However, no online service is 100% secure, and we can't guarantee absolute security.

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The "last updated" date at the top of this page will reflect any changes.

9. Complaints

If you have a privacy complaint, contact us first at admin@bakerproduction.com. If you're not satisfied with our response, you can contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner at oaic.gov.au.
